P&L calculator — you accept the imbalance

You sold this quantity, can't deliver, and you just accept the imbalance — no buying back, no struggling. This is simply what the settlement does to your money: settlement = −imbalance price × MW × hours. Red = the price was positive, so you pay it. Green = the price went negative, so the TSO pays you. Expect mostly red: Serbia's imbalance price is positive ~85 % of the time, so accepting it usually costs something — the question is only how much, and whether it beat the alternative (switch the dropdown to compare against a buy-back). Assumption: one delivery = one day over the chosen hours; each row below is a real historical day, and the headline numbers use a typical (average) day. Date range comes from the filters above. The same MW is held in every chosen hour (it's a power), so one day = quantity × hours of energy — e.g. 50 MW over 5 h = 250 MWh.

You sold this quantity and can't deliver. Two ways out: buy it back on the IDM, or accept the imbalance and let it settle. This shows which one was cheaper: P&L = (IDM buy-back price − imbalance price) × MW × hours. Green = accepting the imbalance was cheaper — you made money by staying in imbalance. Red = you should have bought it back. The price you originally sold at doesn't appear — you were paid it either way, so it cancels out; only buy-back vs imbalance moves your money.
